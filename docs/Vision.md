# Vision

Product description only. No implementation details.

## Problem

Young children are often given apps that:

- Reward speed over thought
- Reveal answers too quickly
- Use manipulative engagement (streaks, timers, pressure, dopamine loops)
- Jump to advanced puzzles (like Sudoku) before foundational reasoning skills exist

Parents and educators need a calm tool that builds **how to think**, not how to tap faster.

## Target audience

**Primary:** Children aged 3–7

**Secondary:** Parents and caregivers who want:

- Screen time that feels purposeful
- Visible growth in reasoning skills
- A calm experience without guilt or overstimulation

Age bands are recommendations only. Placement follows concept mastery.

## Learning philosophy

Montessori-inspired and discovery-based:

- Teach reasoning, not memorization
- Encourage discovery instead of direct instruction
- Treat mistakes as learning opportunities
- Never reveal answers
- Reward curiosity, not speed
- Prefer calm interaction over stimulation

The app adapts to the child. The child does not adapt to the app.

### Skills we develop

1. Observation
2. Pattern recognition
3. Logical thinking
4. Deduction
5. Persistence
6. Independent problem solving

### Learning journey (high level)

Matching → Sorting → Same/Different → Patterns → Sequences → Rows → Columns → Elimination → Deduction → Mini Sudoku → 4×4 → 6×6 → 9×9 Sudoku

Detailed educational specs live in `Curriculum.md`.

## Child experience

- Soft colors, warm and minimal UI
- Large touch targets
- Calm animations
- Hints that ask questions, never give answers
- Space to try, fail gently, and try again
- No shame, no countdown pressure, no manipulative rewards

## Parent experience

- Trust that the app is educational, not addictive by design
- Clear sense of what the child is practicing (concepts, not points)
- Progress that reflects mastery, not playtime length
- Simple controls and transparency over what the child sees

## Competitor research (positioning)

Typical kids puzzle / “brain” apps often emphasize:

- High stimulation and gamification
- Leaderboards, coins, streaks
- Content volume over pedagogical sequence

Little Logic Lab differentiates by:

- Explicit anti-gamification stance for V1 and beyond (unless philosophy changes in the Decision Log)
- Curriculum-first sequencing toward Sudoku-like reasoning
- Question-based hints
- Calm Montessori aesthetic and interaction model

## Success metrics

Prefer learning-aligned signals over vanity engagement:

- Concept mastery progression along the curriculum path
- Independent completion rates (with hints used as scaffolding, not crutches)
- Persistence after mistakes (retry without quitting)
- Parent-reported calm / low-friction sessions
- Low reliance on answer-revealing behaviors (we do not ship answer reveal)

Non-goals as success metrics:

- Session length maximization
- Daily streak pressure
- Ad or IAP engagement loops
